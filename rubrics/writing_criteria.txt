WRITING QUALITY CRITERIA
 
THESIS AND ARGUMENTATION
A strong thesis clearly states the writer's position and is supported
throughout the essay. It is specific and arguable, not a restatement
of the prompt. The ASAP rubric rewards essays that develop a clear
point of view and demonstrate critical thinking.
 
EVIDENCE FROM SOURCE TEXTS
Essays in ASAP must use evidence taken from the source texts provided.
Strong essays integrate specific quotes, paraphrases, or details from
the source material and explain how the evidence supports the argument.
Weak essays make claims without referencing the source texts.
 
ORGANISATION AND STRUCTURE
Introduction: hook, context, clear thesis.
Body paragraphs: topic sentence, evidence from source, analysis.
Conclusion: restatement, synthesis, closing thought.
Transitions connect ideas between paragraphs.
 
LANGUAGE AND STYLE
Score 6: skillful, varied, accurate vocabulary. Meaningful sentence variety.
Score 4-5: appropriate vocabulary with some variety.
Score 2-3: limited vocabulary, weak sentence structure.
Score 1: fundamental vocabulary errors, severe sentence flaws.
 
GRAMMAR AND MECHANICS
Score 6: free of most errors in grammar, usage, and mechanics.
Score 4-5: generally free of errors.
Score 2-3: accumulation of errors affecting meaning.
Score 1: pervasive errors persistently interfering with meaning.
